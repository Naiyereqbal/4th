A.P.J.ABDUL KALAM

He was known to be the “missle man” for his work on the development of science and technology.


He was a well-known aerospace scientist

Dr. A.P.J Abdul Kalam was the 11th President of India

A.P.J. Abdul Kalam served as president of the Republic of India from 2002 to 2007

He was born on October 15, 1931 in a town called Rameswaram in Tamil Nadu

He was a beloved leader and was known as a people's president

He had played an important role in the Pokhran II nuclear test in 1998

The Government of India awarded him with the highest civilian award Bharat Ratna in 1997

He is an inspiration to the youth of India

he successfully led the development of Agni and Prithvi, the indigenous guided missiles

On July 27, 2015, Dr. Kalam passed away while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong
